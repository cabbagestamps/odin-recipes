Aloo Gobi

Vegetarian | 1 hour

Bursting with flavour, this nutritious meal will quickly become a staple. Best served as part of a larger meal with wetter curries, garlic dip, and pappadums, as this is a relatively dry version of Aloo Gobi.

#Ingredients

#Method

Ingredients
  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 cauliflower, cut into small florets
  • 1 onion
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp cummin seeds
  • 1.5 tsp ginger & garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp tumeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp dry mango powder (amchur)
  • 1/4 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Oil

Method
  1. Preheat fanforced oven to 200 degrees Celsius
  2. In a large bowl, mix potato and cauliflower with oil and salt, cook in oven for 40-50 minutes
  3. In a large pot, heat oil, add cumin seeds and let crackle
  4. Add onion and cook until translucent
  5. Add ginger & garlic, cook for 2 minutes
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, cook for 2 minutes
  7. Add tumeric, chili, coriander, amchur
  8. Cover pot and cook for 2-3 minutes
  9. Add vegies, mix
  10. Add garam masala, cook for 5-6 minutes
  11. Cook further to attain preferred dryness, add water to mkae more wet
  12. Serve with steamed rice (garlic dip and pappadums optional)
